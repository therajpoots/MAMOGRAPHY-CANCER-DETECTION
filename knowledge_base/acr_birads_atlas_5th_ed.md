# ACR BI-RADS Atlas (5th Edition) - Breast Ultrasound Reference Guidelines

The Breast Imaging-Reporting and Data System (BI-RADS) is a standardized lexicon developed by the American College of Radiology (ACR). It provides a unified system for describing breast ultrasound findings and categorizing the corresponding likelihood of malignancy.

## Section 1: Standardized Ultrasound Lexicon

### 1. Shape
- **Oval**: Elliptical, egg-shaped, or configuration with gentle undulations (typically benign).
- **Round**: Circular, spherical, or disk-like.
- **Irregular**: Neither round nor oval; possesses irregular, jagged, or complex contours (associated with malignancy).

### 2. Orientation
- **Parallel**: The long axis of the lesion lies parallel to the skin line ("wider-than-tall"). Commonly associated with benign structures (e.g., simple cysts or fibroadenomas).
- **Non-Parallel**: The long axis is perpendicular to the skin line ("taller-than-wide"). Highly specific indicator of malignancy, representing invasive growth patterns.

### 3. Margin Descriptors
- **Circumscribed**: A well-defined boundary with a sharp transition between the lesion and surrounding tissue.
- **Non-Circumscribed**:
  - *Indistinct*: Lack of clear demarcation between the mass and surrounding parenchyma.
  - *Angular*: Jagged, sharp, or irregular edges forming acute angles.
  - *Microlobulated*: Margin characterized by small, repeating cycles or scalloped loops.
  - *Spiculated*: Radiating lines projecting outwards from the center of the mass (highest positive predictive value for invasive ductal carcinoma).

### 4. Lesion Boundary
- **Abrupt Interface**: Immediate transition between the margin of the mass and normal tissue.
- **Echogenic Halo**: A bright, hyper-reflective boundary zone indicating a desmoplastic response or infiltration into surrounding parenchymal structures.

### 5. Echo Pattern
- **Anechoic**: Absence of internal echoes (fluid-filled, typical of simple cysts).
- **Hypoechoic**: Internal reflections are darker/lower intensity than surrounding fat tissue.
- **Hyperechoic**: Internal reflections are brighter than surrounding fat tissue.
- **Complex Cystic and Solid**: Solid elements showing internal echoes coupled with fluid-filled anechoic regions.

## Section 2: BI-RADS Categories and Malignancy Risks

Below are the official ACR BI-RADS categories, their clinical descriptions, risk rates, and standard management actions:

### BI-RADS Category 0: Incomplete Assessment
- **Clinical Scenario**: Inadequate study or need for comparison with historical studies.
- **Risk of Malignancy**: N/A
- **Management Recommendation**: Retrieve priors or perform supplemental diagnostic imaging (mammography, tomosynthesis, or MRI).

### BI-RADS Category 1: Negative
- **Clinical Scenario**: Symmetrical breast tissue, no masses, structural distortions, or calcifications present.
- **Risk of Malignancy**: ~0%
- **Management Recommendation**: Routine annual screening.

### BI-RADS Category 2: Benign Finding
- **Clinical Scenario**: Solid masses with benign features (simple cysts, stable simple fibroadenomas, intramammary lymph nodes).
- **Risk of Malignancy**: ~0%
- **Management Recommendation**: Routine annual screening.

### BI-RADS Category 3: Probably Benign Finding
- **Clinical Scenario**: Solid mass with oval shape, parallel orientation, and circumscribed margins.
- **Risk of Malignancy**: >0% but <= 2%
- **Management Recommendation**: Short-interval diagnostic follow-up (typically diagnostic mammogram or ultrasound in 6 months, then at 12 and 24 months to confirm stability).

### BI-RADS Category 4: Suspicious Abnormality
- **Clinical Scenario**: Findings lack classic benign features and present moderate elements suspicious of malignancy. Tissue biopsy is recommended.
- **Subdivisions and Risk Profiles**:
  - **Category 4A (Low Suspicion)**: Malignancy risk >2% to <= 10%.
  - **Category 4B (Moderate Suspicion)**: Malignancy risk >10% to <= 50%.
  - **Category 4C (High Suspicion)**: Malignancy risk >50% to <95%.
- **Management Recommendation**: Core needle biopsy under ultrasound or stereotactic guidance.

### BI-RADS Category 5: Highly Suggestive of Malignancy
- **Clinical Scenario**: Solid masses with irregular shapes, non-parallel orientation, spiculated margins, and acoustic shadowing.
- **Risk of Malignancy**: >= 95%
- **Management Recommendation**: Core needle biopsy and immediate referral for surgical or oncology consultation.

### BI-RADS Category 6: Known Biopsy-Proven Malignancy
- **Clinical Scenario**: Confirmed histology of malignancy prior to surgical excision or neoadjuvant chemotherapy.
- **Risk of Malignancy**: N/A (100% confirmed)
- **Management Recommendation**: Surgical resection, staging, and systemic/radiation therapeutic planning.
