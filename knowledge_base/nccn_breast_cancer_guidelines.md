# NCCN Clinical Practice Guidelines in Oncology: Breast Cancer Screening and Diagnosis

This document contains recommendations synthesized from the National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN) Guidelines for Breast Cancer Screening and Diagnosis (Version 2.2026).

## Section 1: Screening Recommendations for Average-Risk Women

1. **Definition of Average Risk**:
   - Lifetime risk of breast cancer < 15-20% based on models (e.g. Gail model).
   - No history of prior thoracic radiation therapy (between ages 10-30).
   - No known pathogenic genetic mutations (e.g., BRCA1, BRCA2, TP53, PTEN).

2. **Screening Modality & Frequency**:
   - **Age >= 40**: Clinical breast exam combined with annual screening mammography or digital breast tomosynthesis (DBT).
   - **Supplemental Screening**: For women with dense breasts (Density Categories C and D), supplemental screening with handheld or automated breast ultrasound (ABUS) or breast MRI is recommended as an adjunct to mammography.

## Section 2: Clinical Diagnostic Pathways for Detected Lesions

### 1. Solid / Solid-Cystic Masses on Ultrasound
- **BI-RADS 1 & 2 (Negative / Benign)**:
  - Routine screening annually. No further follow-up or biopsy.
- **BI-RADS 3 (Probably Benign)**:
  - Clinical management: Diagnostic ultrasound in 6 months, then every 6–12 months for up to 2 years to demonstrate stable morphology.
  - If a BI-RADS 3 mass increases in size by >= 20% in diameter or changes to include non-circumscribed margins or non-parallel orientation, it must be upgraded to BI-RADS 4 and biopsied.
  - Patient preference or anxiety can be an indication for core biopsy in BI-RADS 3 cases.
- **BI-RADS 4A, 4B, 4C & 5 (Suspicious to Highly Suggestive)**:
  - Clinical management: Core needle biopsy using ultrasound guidance is the gold standard for establishing tissue diagnosis.
  - Fine Needle Aspiration (FNA) is NOT recommended as the primary diagnostic tool due to inability to differentiate between in situ and invasive disease, and high rates of non-diagnostic cellular yield.
  - In cases of Category 5 masses, a negative core biopsy result is considered discordant, and immediate surgical consultation/excision is required.

## Section 3: High-Risk Screening Protocols

1. **Prior Thoracic Radiation (Ages 10-30)**:
   - Annual screening mammography/DBT and annual breast MRI starting 8 years after radiation therapy or at age 25 (whichever is later).
2. **Genetic Mutation Carriers (BRCA1, BRCA2, TP53, PTEN)**:
   - Annual breast MRI starting at age 25.
   - Annual mammography/DBT starting at age 30.
3. **Personal History of Atypical Hyperplasia (ADH, ALH, LCIS)**:
   - Annual screening mammography.
   - Supplemental breast MRI should be considered based on overall calculated lifetime risk.
